# Sources

Research and reference sources used for ExploreBarbizon content.

Last updated: 2026-03-28

---

## Primary — in active use

### grappilles.fr
**URL:** https://www.grappilles.fr  
**Type:** Local historical archive  
**Language:** French  
Historical archive of Barbizon built by Luigi's father-in-law, drawing on municipal records, period literature, old newspapers, and cartographic materials. Treated as a primary research source and formally credited on the platform. The most authoritative source for village-level history.

### barbizon.fr
**URL:** https://www.barbizon.fr  
**Type:** Official municipal website  
**Language:** French  
Official site of the Mairie de Barbizon. Used for confirmed institutional data, museum listings, and local governance history.

### barbizontourisme.fr
**URL:** https://www.barbizontourisme.fr  
**Type:** Tourist office  
**Language:** French  
Office de tourisme de Barbizon. Used for current practical information and confirmed addresses.

### fontainebleau-tourisme.com
**URL:** https://www.fontainebleau-tourisme.com/fr/barbizon  
**Type:** Regional tourism body  
**Language:** French  
Covers Barbizon within the wider Fontainebleau area. Useful for cross-referencing trail and heritage information.

### fontainebleau-photo.fr
**URL:** https://fontainebleau-photo.fr  
**Type:** Visual archive  
**Language:** French  
Photographic and postcard archive of the Fontainebleau area. Used for historical visual material.

### cpbarbizon.wordpress.com
**URL:** https://cpbarbizon.wordpress.com  
**Type:** Postcard blog  
**Language:** French  
Postcard-focused blog with historical views of the village. Secondary source for period visual documentation.

## Institutional — high authority

### Musée des Peintres de Barbizon
**URL:** https://www.musee-peintres-barbizon.fr  
**Type:** Departmental museum  
**Language:** French  
Official site of the museum housed in the Auberge Ganne and Théodore Rousseau's studio. The institutional authority on École de Barbizon history, with permanent collections and scholarly exhibition texts.

### Archives Départementales de Seine-et-Marne
**URL:** https://archives.seine-et-marne.fr  
**Type:** Departmental archive  
**Language:** French / English  
Digitised civil records, cadastral maps, postcards, and municipal monographs. Includes dedicated Barbizon sections and the Félix Herbet fond (Fontainebleau/Barbizon research papers). Searchable by commune at archives.seine-et-marne.fr/fr/recherche-par-commune.

### Musée d'Orsay — collections & essays
**URL:** https://www.musee-orsay.fr  
**Type:** National museum  
**Language:** French / English  
Holds major Barbizon school works. Curatorial essays and online catalogue provide authoritative artist biographies and artwork-specific historical context for Millet, Rousseau, Daubigny, and others.

### Metropolitan Museum of Art — Heilbrunn Timeline
**URL:** https://www.metmuseum.org/essays/the-barbizon-school-french-painters-of-nature  
**Type:** Museum essay  
**Language:** English  
Scholarly essay on the Barbizon school with detailed historical context. Useful for English-language narratives about the art movement and its relationship to the forest and village.

### Ministère de la Culture — Base Mérimée / POP
**URL:** https://pop.culture.gouv.fr  
**Type:** National heritage database  
**Language:** French  
Official heritage inventory. Barbizon's Auberge Ganne and other listed monuments have notice sheets with precise addresses, historical descriptions, and protection status. Canonical source for confirmed factual data on listed buildings.

## Digitised primary texts — public domain

### Internet Archive — 19th-century Barbizon texts
**URL:** https://archive.org  
**Type:** Digital library  
**Language:** English / French  
Full-text access to out-of-copyright books including:
- Thomson, *The Barbizon School of Painters* (1890)
- Mollett, *Painters of Barbizon: Millet, Rousseau, Diaz* 
- Hoeber, *The Barbizon Painters: Being the Story of the Men of Thirty*

Rich primary source material for artist biographies and period descriptions of the village and forest.

### Gallica — BnF digital library
**URL:** https://gallica.bnf.fr  
**Type:** National digital library  
**Language:** French  
Bibliothèque nationale de France's digitised collection. Searchable historical French press (including 19th-century coverage of Barbizon artists), guidebooks, maps, and photographs. Essential French-language primary source.

## Forest & nature

### ONF — Office National des Forêts
**URL:** https://www.onf.fr  
**Type:** Public forestry body  
**Language:** French  
Official body managing Fontainebleau forest. Authoritative source for confirmed trail data, protected zone information, geological descriptions, and the history of forest management including Denecourt's legacy.

### Société des Amis de la Forêt de Fontainebleau (AFF)
**URL:** https://www.foret-de-fontainebleau.org  
**Type:** Conservation association  
**Language:** French  
Long-running research and conservation association. Publishes research on flora, fauna, geology, and the human history of the forest — including the sandstone quarries and Denecourt's trail system. Important source for the Forest & Nature layer.

## Usage principles

- **Only factual, confirmed data enters the database.** Sources are used to verify — not to speculate.
- **grappilles.fr** is the primary narrative provenance for village-level historical content and is formally credited on the platform.
- **Base Mérimée / POP** is the canonical source for addresses and protection status of listed buildings.
- **Archives de Seine-et-Marne** is the institutional counterpart to grappilles.fr for cadastral and civil record verification.
- When a source cannot confirm a piece of data, the data is not inserted. See `brain/decisions.md` for the factual integrity principle.
